C Corporations

The following are the advantages of a C corporation:

  • Limits your liability if you get sued. In other words, if someone sues you, they can't reach your personal assets.
  • Attractive to venture capital firms and other investors.
  • The usual type of entity if you expect to go public.

The following are the disadvantages of a C corporation:

  • Taxed at the entity level. This means the corporation pays tax on any profits and then you will also pay tax on any distributions of profits you receive from the corporation.
  • Requires more paperwork and other corporate formalities than LLCs.

Not legal advice. Speak to a business lawyer to determine which entity is best for your particular situation.
