Limited Liability Companies

The following are the advantages of a LLC:

  • Limits your liability if you get sued. In other words, if someone sues you, they can't reach your personal assets.
  • Not taxed at the entity level. So the corporation does not pay any tax on its profits. You still pay tax your share of the company's profits.
  • Very easy to set up and maintain. Less paperwork and corporate formalities than a C or S corporation.

The following are the disadvantages of an LLC:

  • Will need to be converted to a C corporation before you go public.
  • May require you to pay more in self-employment taxes than an S corporation. Speak to a lawyer or accountant for more details.

Not legal advice. Speak to a business lawyer to determine which entity is best for your particular situation.
