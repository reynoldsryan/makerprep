S Corporations

The following are the advantages of a S corporation:

  • Limits your liability if you get sued. In other words, if someone sues you, they can't reach your personal assets.
  • Not taxed at the entity level. So the corporation does not pay any tax on its profits. You still pay tax on distributions of profits to you.
  • The usual type of entity for small businesses without many owners. You may be able to pay less self=employment taxes than with other entity types. Speak to a lawyer or accountant for more details.

The following are the disadvantages of a S corporation:

  • Can only have a limited (100) number of owners.
  • Requires an election be made with the IRS.

Not legal advice. Speak to a business lawyer to determine which entity is best for your particular situation.
